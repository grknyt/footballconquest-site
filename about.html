CHAPTER 03 · THE STORY

About this project.

Football Conquest started as a tool to make better videos. It turned into something the community could play with too.

Who made this

Hi, I'm Gürkan. I run two YouTube channels focused on offline EA Sports FC content:

  • @Ghiellini — English-language career-mode and simulation videos.
  • @GRKN7 — The same content for the Turkish-language audience.

Combined, the channels reach more than 700,000 subscribers who care a lot about offline FC content: career-mode runs, club rebuilds, World Cup simulations, and the kind of Imperialism format you're holding in your hand right now.

Why we built it

The Imperialism format — a single team trying to conquer the world one match at a time — has been a fan favourite for years. But making it on video was hard. You needed Photoshop, a 30,000-pixel map file, and a lot of patience between matches to manually paint conquered territory and reposition flags.

This simulator does all of that automatically. Pick your team, play the matches in FC 26, enter the scores, and the map updates itself. What used to take 20 minutes between matches now takes 20 seconds.

Then it occurred to me: if this is useful for me as a creator, it's probably fun for fans too. Free in your browser, no install, no sign-up. Pick a team and see how far you can take them.

The format, codified

Football Imperialism as a format has loose rules — different creators do it different ways. Football Conquest codifies a specific ruleset:

  • Random-direction arrow for opponent selection
  • FIFA Men's World Ranking points as the strength baseline
  • Elo-style probability for match outcomes
  • Three losses to the same opponent ends the campaign
  • Losing means retreating to your previous conquest
  • Match scorelines weighted by the strength gap

The rules aren't arbitrary — they're tuned to produce stories. Close matchups go to extra time more often. Underdogs winning produce tight 1–0 scorelines, not blowouts. Big empires can absorb a couple of losses before collapsing.

What's next

This is a starting point. On the roadmap:

  • Mobile apps for Android and iOS, so you can play on the go.
  • Club leagues as Pro unlocks — Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga, Süper Lig — with fictional but recognisable club names you can rename to your favourites.
  • Sharable result pages — when your campaign ends, get a URL that anyone can visit to see your final map and turn-by-turn history.
  • More languages — Spanish and Arabic next, then whichever audiences ask for it.
